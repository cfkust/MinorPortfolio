About Me

I’m a senior at the University of Maryland studying Computer Science with a minor in Digital Storytelling and Poetics. While I was first drawn to computer science for its logic and problem-solving, I realized quickly I was interested in how technology could be used to facilitate communication and interaction with users; to communicate ideas, tell stories, and shape user experience.

I chose to pursue a minor in Digital Storytelling and Poetics because I wanted to explore more creative ways of interacting with literature and ideas in a digital space. In a world where so much of our reading, learning, and communication happens online, I became curious about how design, structure, and interactivity shape meaning. This minor gave me the space to think critically about narrative, audience, and expression, and helped me approach technology as a medium for storytelling rather than just a technical tool.

The work collected in this portfolio reflects that balance. Across projects involving web development, interactive media, and digital storytelling, I focus on making complex ideas more accessible and engaging. My background in computer science has trained me to think precisely and systematically, while my work in digital storytelling has taught me to slow down, consider user experience, and communicate with intention. Together, these skills shape how I design, write, and build in digital environments.

Looking forward, I hope to pursue a career that allows me to blend technical development with thoughtful, human-centered design. Whether I am writing, coding, or building interactive experiences, I am most interested in creating digital spaces that are not only functional, but meaningful.

Featured Works

Echoes of Absence

An interactive Twine mystery where readers join Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson to investigate missing children, making choices that shape the story and reveal different clues and outcomes. It demonstrates how digital storytelling turns traditional narratives into immersive, user-driven experiences.

Content Analysis & Creation

This project explores how social media platforms like Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook communicate complex issues by analyzing content, structure, and audience engagement. The analysis informed the creation of two Facebook advocacy posts that utilize similar rhetorical strategies.

Figma

This project is a mobile app prototype for the No Arena Philadelphia campaign, inspired by my concerns about the Philadelphia Chinatown and community displacement. It redesigns an existing website into a mobile experience centered on the most impactful, user-driven actions.
