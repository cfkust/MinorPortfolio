Project Overview

This project is an interactive narrative created using Twine, a tool that supports nonlinear and choice-driven storytelling. Titled Echoes of Absence: The Mystery of the Missing Children, it reimagines a Sherlock Holmes style mystery in a digital format and reflects my interest in combining storytelling with interactive design.

I have long been interested in mystery narratives, particularly the deductive reasoning and atmosphere present in Sherlock Holmes stories. In this project, I wanted to examine how that experience changes when the reader is no longer a passive observer but an active participant. By making choices throughout the story, readers take on a more engaged role in uncovering clues and shaping the direction of the investigation.

During the development process, I focused on how interactivity can enhance reader engagement. Elements such as branching paths, sound, and visual imagery were incorporated to create a more immersive experience while maintaining the tone and structure of a traditional detective narrative.

This project also reflects my interest in remixing, which involves drawing from existing works and adapting them into new forms. By taking inspiration from Sherlock Holmes and translating it into an interactive medium, I explored how familiar narratives can be reinterpreted within digital environments.
