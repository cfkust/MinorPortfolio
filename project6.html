Project Overview

This project presents an interactive storytelling platform that allows users to shape a narrative through their inputs. Instead of a fixed, linear story, users navigate a story world by interacting with clickable elements, with the narrative and visuals dynamically adapting to their choices. This creates a personalized and immersive experience.

Drawing on hypertext fiction and interactive media, the project emphasizes user agency and nonlinear narrative structures. It challenges traditional ideas of authorship by positioning meaning as something co-created through interaction between the system and the user. Within digital textuality, the project highlights how digital texts can become adaptive, multimodal, and participatory. By combining text, visuals, and interactivity, it demonstrates how storytelling can shift from static to dynamic, creating a more engaging relationship between user and narrative.

The project involves two parts: a trailer and an interactive story. The trailer introduces the interactive story by highlighting its key themes, visuals, and user-driven narrative experience. It is designed to engage the audience and communicate how the project invites participation while framing expectations for the full work.
