Project Overview

This project explores experimental writing and poetic forms within digital textuality and electronic literature. It incorporates techniques such as constrained and found writing to examine the relationship between traditional literary practices and digital media.

The work emphasizes interactive, multimodal, and participatory elements, using digital tools to expand literary expression and enhance reader engagement. An accompanying artist’s statement reflects on creative choices and the intersection of traditional and digital practices.

Poem 1

My first work is a found poem with phrases taken from a poem by Erin Hanson.

Original Poem

“There is freedom waiting for you,
On the breezes of the sky,
And you ask "What if I fall?"
Oh but my darling,
What if you fly?”
― Erin Hanson

Found Poem: Oh, My Darling

Oh, my darling
there on the sky
you ask if you fall
if freedom is there

Oh, my darling
if you fall I fall
fall on the breezes
of what is waiting

Oh, my darling
you ask what is waiting,
waiting for you
for you there is sky

Oh, my darling
you ask what is sky
freedom is waiting
for if you fall, you fly

Poem 2

My second poem is a constrained poem where I don’t use the letter e. The reason I chose this letter is because it is the most used vowel in the English language which would make creating the poem more difficult.

Constrained Poem: Spring Again

Soft winds blow by
Gold rays kiss a warm sky
Spring stirs, bold and proud
Chill lifts at dawn’s soft sigh

Cold dusk turns to light
Buds push up from rich, dark ground
Sunlit air swirls with warmth
Songbirds trill with a charming sound

Clouds part, soft and slow
A world stirs with morning song
Grass sways, dancing with wind
Bright light shining all day long

Poem 3

My third poem is a poem where all words of each line begin with the same letter.

Constrained Poem: Nature’s Night

Branches bend, birds boast below,
Crystal currents carve cliffs,
Dancing daffodils drink dew droplets,
Eagles echo eastward emerging elegantly,
Fluttering fireflies flicker free,
Golden grass gently gathering gusts,
Jubilant jackrabbits jump joyfully,
Lush leaves lull lazy lakes,
Misty meadows murmur melodies,
Purple petals paint peaceful pastures,
Velvet violets veil valleys,
Wandering winds weave whispers,
Night’s nectar nestles neatly,

Artist Statement

The three poems I wrote each focus on creating vivid imagery of nature while adhering to different constraints, which shaped the process and outcome of each. In my first poem, I used a found poem technique, selecting words from a smaller poem and transforming them into something new. The repetitive use of "oh my darling" allowed the poem to feel like a dialogue, creating a rhythm and flow that enhanced its reflective nature. Despite being limited to the same words, the underlying theme of nature remained intact, offering a sense of continuity across the piece. The second poem introduced a more challenging constraint, where I avoided using words with the letter "e." This forced me to carefully consider every word choice, which led to a more deliberate, thoughtful approach. The result was a poem that, while restricted in vocabulary, still conveyed a vivid picture of nature through the purposeful selection of words and careful construction.

The third poem involved starting each line with the same letter, and I chose a noun related to nature for each line to create cohesion within the poem. This constraint led to a different type of imagery, as I focused heavily on adjectives to describe each noun. The challenge of finding words that fit this pattern pushed me to explore the language more creatively, and I ended up with a piece where the descriptive imagery stood out. Despite the varying constraints, all three poems maintained a consistent theme of nature and beauty, with each piece offering a unique take on how language can shape imagery. Together, they highlight how different constraints can lead to varied, yet cohesive, creative expressions of the same core theme.
